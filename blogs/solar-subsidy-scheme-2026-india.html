What is Solar Subsidy Scheme 2026 in India

The main solar subsidy program in India is the PM Surya Ghar Muft Bijli Yojana, launched in 2024 with a budget of about ₹75,000 crore to promote rooftop solar installations across India. [1]

The scheme aims to install rooftop solar systems in 1 crore households by 2027. [3]

Key Benefits

  • Up to ₹78,000 subsidy
  • Up to 300 units free electricity per month
  • Lower electricity bills
  • Opportunity to sell excess electricity to the grid

[4]

Solar Subsidy Amount 2026

The subsidy depends on the solar system capacity installed at your home.

Solar System Size Government Subsidy
1 kW ₹30,000
2 kW ₹60,000
3 kW or above ₹78,000

[2]

Eligibility for Solar Subsidy

To apply for solar subsidy in India, you must meet these requirements:

  • Must be an Indian citizen
  • Must own a residential house
  • Roof must have space for solar panels
  • Must have a valid electricity connection
  • Should not have already taken another solar subsidy

[1]

How to Apply for Solar Subsidy in India

The application process is simple.

Step-by-Step Process

  1. Visit the official solar portal
  2. Register with your electricity connection details
  3. Choose a government approved solar vendor
  4. Install rooftop solar system
  5. Apply for subsidy claim
  6. Subsidy amount will be credited to your bank account

The subsidy is transferred directly to the customer's bank account through Direct Benefit Transfer (DBT). [5]

Solar System Cost After Subsidy

Example for a typical residential system:

Solar System Price Subsidy Final Cost
3 kW Solar System ₹1.8 – ₹2.2 lakh ₹78,000 ₹1 – ₹1.4 lakh

Because of this subsidy, many homeowners recover their solar investment within 3-5 years. [6]

Can You Sell Extra Solar Power

Yes. With net metering, you can sell extra electricity generated by your solar system to the electricity board.

This reduces your electricity bill and sometimes generates additional income.
