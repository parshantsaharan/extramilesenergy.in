Introduction: Could a 'Salt Battery' Power Your Next Electric Car?

Imagine a battery that works at 90% power even in extreme cold, charges to 80% in just 15 minutes, and has almost zero fire risk. This isn't science fiction, but the reality of Sodium-Ion Battery technology.

China's CATL (Contemporary Amperex Technology Co. Limited), the world's largest battery manufacturer, launched its branded sodium-ion battery "Naxtra" in April 2025. This technology will be available in passenger vehicles from 2026 and could be a game-changer for the EV industry.

1. What is a Sodium-Ion Battery? The Basics

A Sodium-Ion Battery is a rechargeable battery where Sodium (Na) ions carry energy instead of Lithium. The biggest advantage of Sodium is that it is approximately 1000 times more abundant on Earth than Lithium ‚Äì easily available from salt. This makes the battery cheaper and the supply chain more stable.

How does it work?

  • Charging: When external power is applied, sodium ions move from the cathode and accumulate at the anode.
  • Discharging: During use, these ions move back to the cathode, creating an electric current.

5. Importance for India & Future Challenges

India currently depends on lithium imports for over 90% of its needs, creating instability for the EV industry. Sodium-ion technology offers a chance to break this dependency.

Progress in India

  • IISc Bangalore: Research on Sodium-Ion batteries.
  • Amar Raja Group: Investment in advanced battery tech.
  • National Mission: Government policy support.

Remaining Challenges

  • Energy Density: Still not enough for high-performance EVs.
  • Manufacturing Scale: Beginning of mass production.
  • Infrastructure: Charging, recycling network development.

Market Forecast

Experts estimate that by 2030, Sodium-Ion batteries will capture 10-15% of the global market, especially in energy storage and affordable EVs. India stands to benefit the most from this technology.
